Current and Future Impact of Extreme Temperatures on Mortality Rates

  • Indirect Causes of Temperature-Related Deaths: Most fatalities from extreme temperatures result from conditions like cardiovascular disease, kidney disease, and respiratory infections, rather than direct heat stroke or hypothermia.
  • Sub-Optimal Temperatures and Premature Deaths: Extreme temperatures contribute to a significant number of premature deaths each year, though they rarely appear directly on death certificates.
  • Vulnerability of Older Adults: Individuals over 65 are especially at risk, with extreme temperatures exacerbating existing health issues and leading to earlier deaths.
  • Research Insights: Studies indicate that temperature extremes can reduce lifespans by at least one year, with excess death rates helping to estimate the impact of extreme temperatures.
